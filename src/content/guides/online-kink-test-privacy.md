Privacy matters because kink test answers can feel personal even when the questions are broad. Before using any online test, look for signs that the site respects sensitive reflection.

The safest simple approach is local scoring. That means the page calculates your result in your browser instead of sending answers to a server account or saved profile. This site uses local-only scoring for the quiz experience.

This site may use Google Analytics to understand aggregate page use. The quiz answers themselves are not submitted, saved to an account, or attached to a report.

## Privacy signals to check

Look for clear language near the quiz, not just hidden in a policy page. A trustworthy tool should tell you whether answers are saved, uploaded, connected to an account, or used for analytics.

Also notice whether the test asks for unnecessary information. A basic reflection quiz does not need your email, legal name, relationship status, exact location, or social account.

## What local scoring means

Local scoring means your answer values are held in the page while you take the test. The result is generated from those values on your device. If you refresh or leave, the quiz does not preserve a saved report.

Local scoring does not make your whole browsing environment private. Your internet provider, browser history, shared device, analytics tools, or workplace network may still reveal that you visited a site. Use a private device and browser mode if that matters to you.

## Sharing results safely

If you share results, share only what you are comfortable naming. A screenshot may include more than you intend. It is often better to summarize themes in your own words.

## FAQ

### Does local scoring save my report?

No. The result is generated in the browser session and is not saved to an account.

### Is private browsing still useful?

It can reduce local history on a shared device, but it does not hide traffic from every network layer.

### Should I use my real name?

This quiz does not ask for a name. Sensitive reflection tools should avoid unnecessary identifiers.
